I am a big fan of BBC's show Sherlock. However, the design of John Watson's Blog didn't leave as much of an impression on me. I decided to modernize it a bit by giving it a facelift, making it responsive and trying to improve the experience of reading the blog.

This was a personal project, which I started with the intent of getting to know the WordPress platform and building a website on top of it.

The homepage features the blog posts in chronological order. Only the date, title and excerpt are shown at first, to make wading through content easier. Clicking the Read Post button shows the full post, but does not redirect to a new page, so jumping to the next post after you're done reading the current one is as easy as scrolling down.

The sidebar is retractable, to give the user a choice of either keeping it open and having the navigation always available, or to close it and focus on the content of the blog. This of course isn't available on smaller screens due to not having enough screen real estate.

Transitions

A quick (and dirty) look at some animations that power the interface of the website. These were added to make using the blog a slightly more intuitive and exciting experience.
